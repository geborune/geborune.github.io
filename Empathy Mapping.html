Empathy Mapping

Uncovering your interverviewee

Empathy maps are important because they give designers an avenue into the mind of the customer and help them empathize with their experience, desires, and needs.
They’re also useful for taking insights you gain from user research, digging deeper, and applying them to find concrete solutions. How do you use persona empathy mapping?

To create an empathy map, start with a direct statement, from user interviews or direct feedback.
From there, you create a constellation of thoughts, feelings, and actions that underly the customer’s statement and help you understand why they made the statement
and what their underlying motivations are.

Empathy Maps

A typical empathy map includes four quadrants:

  • Say – What the user says about the question asked. Ideally, this section contains real quotes from users recorded during interviews or user testing sessions.
  • Think – What is the interviewee is thinking? What occupies the user’s thoughts? What matters to the user?
  • Feel – This section contains information about the interviewee’s emotional state. What worries the person being interviewed? What does the user get excited about?
    How does the interviewee feel about the experience?
  • Do – What actions does the user take? What actions and behaviors did you notice?
